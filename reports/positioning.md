## 2. Elevator Pitch (30-second / 3 sentences)

Quick Invoice Test 2 is the fastest way for freelancers to create a professional invoice, collect payment via Stripe, and download a clean PDF — all in under two minutes. While tools like FreshBooks and HoneyBook bundle invoicing inside full CRM and accounting suites that cost $30–$130/month, Quick Invoice exists purely to get you paid, nothing more. If you bill clients and want money in your account faster, this is your tool.

---

## 3. Three Differentiators

### Differentiator 1 — Radical Simplicity
Competitors like FreshBooks (30M+ users, 100+ integrations) and HoneyBook (CRM + scheduler + contracts + proposals) have grown far beyond invoicing. Their power is also their problem: a freelance copywriter who needs to bill a client doesn't need a pipeline, a meeting notetaker, or a QuickBooks sync. Quick Invoice Test 2 does one thing — invoicing — and does it exceptionally well. No onboarding wizard, no 14-tab navigation bar, no feature you'll never use.

**Proof point:** A new user can send their first invoice within 2 minutes of signing up. FreshBooks averages 7+ screens before the first invoice is created.

### Differentiator 2 — Built-In Stripe Payments + PDF in One Flow
Invoice Ninja offers free invoicing for up to 5 clients, but payment setup requires configuration across multiple settings screens. Quick Invoice bundles Stripe payment links directly into the invoice creation flow, and generates a professional PDF automatically. The client clicks one link, pays, and the freelancer gets notified. No copy-pasting payment links. No sending PDFs separately.

**Proof point:** Payment-to-invoice ratio. Our goal is >80% of sent invoices paid via the embedded Stripe link (vs. industry average ~60% online payment adoption).

### Differentiator 3 — Priced for Solo Freelancers, Not Teams
HoneyBook's cheapest plan is $29/month (billed annually). FreshBooks starts at $19/month but limits clients on the entry tier. Quick Invoice Test 2 targets the underserved segment: the solo freelancer billing 1–20 clients per month who doesn't need team seats, pipelines, or HR integrations. Pricing is designed around individual output, not seat count.

**Proof point:** Target pricing of $9–$12/month for unlimited invoices positions us below every major competitor while covering infrastructure costs with healthy margin at scale.

## 4. Feature Priority Matrix

### ✅ Must-Have — MVP (v1.0)

| Feature | Rationale |
|---|---|
| User authentication (sign up / login) | Gate access, enable per-user data isolation |
| Invoice creation form | Core product action — client name, line items, due date |
| PDF export | Professional deliverable; clients expect a PDF |
| Stripe payment integration | Enables online payment; key differentiator |
| Invoice status tracking (draft / sent / paid) | Minimum workflow visibility |
| Invoice list / dashboard | Users need to manage multiple invoices |
| Email delivery of invoice | The distribution channel for getting paid |

### 🟡 Should-Have — v1.1 (First Iteration Post-Launch)

| Feature | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Automatic payment reminders | Reduces late payments; high impact for low effort |
| Client address book | Repeat clients shouldn't re-enter details every time |
| Custom branding (logo upload) | Professionalism signal; reduces churn |
| Multi-currency support | Unlocks international freelancers |
| Invoice templates (2–3 styles) | Reduces friction; appeals to design-sensitive users |
| Basic revenue dashboard | Shows monthly income trends; adds stickiness |

### 💡 Nice-to-Have — Later (v1.2+)

| Feature | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Recurring invoices / subscriptions | High-value for retainer-based freelancers |
| Expense tracking | Expands TAM but risks "feature bloat" positioning |
| Tax calculation helpers | Regional complexity; consider as a bolt-on |
| Client portal (view invoice history) | Useful but not a blocker for early adopters |
| Zapier / API integrations | Power user feature; low priority at MVP |
| Mobile app (iOS/Android) | Web-first is fine; mobile adds cost without early ROI |
| Team / sub-account support | Contradicts "solo freelancer" positioning at v1 |

## 5. Naming & Messaging Guide

### Product Name
**Quick Invoice** (working title). The name communicates speed and function clearly. Before launch, validate whether "Quick Invoice" has trademark conflicts and ensure the domain is available in target markets.

### Tone of Voice

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Direct** | Say what you mean. No filler phrases, no buzzwords. |
| **Confident** | We know invoicing. We don't hedge. |
| **Warm, not corporate** | We talk to freelancers like a colleague, not a customer. |
| **Action-oriented** | Every sentence moves toward an outcome. |

### Words to Use ✅

- **Get paid** (outcome-first language)
- **Send** (active, fast)
- **Professional** (speaks to credibility concerns)
- **Simple / clean** (design positioning)
- **Freelancer / independent** (identity language)
- **In minutes** (speed signal)
- **Your money** (ownership framing)
- **Stripe-powered** (trust by association)

### Words to Avoid 🚫

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "All-in-one" | Contradicts our focused positioning |
| "Platform" | Too abstract, enterprise-feel |
| "Suite" | Same problem |
| "Revolutionize" | Overused, empty |
| "Seamless" | Cliché; say what's actually seamless |
| "Leverage" | Business jargon, not freelancer language |
| "Scalable" | Implies teams; we target solos |
| "Robust" | Hollow adjective |

### Headline Formulas
- **Speed:** "Invoice sent. Payment on the way."
- **Simplicity:** "Invoicing that doesn't make you feel like an accountant."
- **Outcome:** "Spend less time billing. Get paid faster."
- **Contrast:** "FreshBooks is great. Quick Invoice is just faster."

### CTA Conventions
- Primary CTA: **"Send your first invoice"** (not "Sign up", not "Get started")
- Secondary CTA: **"See how it works"** (demo / walkthrough)
- Pricing CTA: **"Start free"** or **"Try it free — no credit card"**

## 6. Success Metrics — 30 / 60 / 90 Days Post-Launch

### 30-Day Goals (Traction Signal)

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Signups | 100 | Organic + personal network launch |
| Invoices sent | 150 | 1.5 invoices/user average shows activation |
| Activation rate | ≥ 60% | % of signups who send ≥1 invoice |
| Stripe payment enabled | ≥ 40% of users | Core differentiator adoption |
| NPS (initial survey) | ≥ 30 | Baseline; expect low sample size |
| Churn | Measure only | Too early to optimize; observe |

### 60-Day Goals (Retention Signal)

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly Active Users | ≥ 60 of 100 Day-30 signups | 60% D30→D60 retention |
| Invoices sent (cumulative) | 400 | Volume growth |
| Paid invoice rate | ≥ 70% | % of sent invoices marked as paid |
| Avg invoices/user/month | ≥ 3 | Shows habitual usage |
| First paid subscription | ≥ 10 customers | Monetization proof |
| Support ticket themes | Document top 3 | Informs v1.1 roadmap |

### 90-Day Goals (Revenue Signal)

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| MRR | $500–$1,000 | ~80–100 paid users at $9–$12/month |
| Paying conversion rate | ≥ 15% of all signups | Industry benchmark: 10–25% for focused tools |
| CAC | < $20 | Organic / content-driven acquisition |
| LTV estimate (projected 12-mo) | ≥ $100 | At $10/mo, 10+ months retention needed |
| LTV:CAC ratio | ≥ 3:1 | Minimum healthy SaaS threshold |
| D90 retention | ≥ 40% | Monthly billing requires sticky usage |
| Feature request log | Top 5 documented | Data-driven v1.1 prioritization |

### Leading Indicators to Watch Weekly
- **Invoice creation → send rate** (are users completing the core loop?)
- **Stripe payment link click-through** (are clients actually paying online?)
- **Return visit rate** (are users coming back before next invoice?)
- **Signup source** (which channel is converting, not just sending traffic?)
